Publishing Policy

The Scientific and Technical Journal “Automobile Roads And Road Construction”, is published by the National Transport University, maintains a certain level of requirements for the selection and reception of articles submitted to the editorial office. These requirements are determined by the scientific directions of the Journal, which are determined by the Certificate of state registration, and the standards of quality of scientific works and their presentation, accepted in the scientific community.

In developing the provisions of the publishing policy of The Scientific and Technical Journal “Automobile Roads And Road Construction”, the editorial board was guided by the recommendations of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE), as well as the experience of foreign and Ukrainian professional communities, scientific organizations and editorial offices.

An essential feature of the professional scientific community is the adoption by scientists and specialists of the Code, which establishes the basic rules of conduct and responsibilities of the community members in relation to each other and to the public. Such a Code is determined by the desire to maximize the benefit of the professional community and limit the actions that could serve the narrow interests of individuals, as well as ensure the right of each author to intellectual property.

In view of the foregoing, the editorial board presents below the set of ethical rules that should be guided by the persons who are involved in the publication of research results in transport construction, related industries and other areas that correspond to the profile of the Journal (editors, authors and reviewers).

The editorial board believes that the following rules are recognized and approved by the majority of qualified researchers, they can also provide substantial assistance to students, postgraduates and young scientists.

The Ethical Code of the Journal editors

1. All materials provided for publication are carefully selected and reviewed. The editorial board reserves the right to reject the article or return it for revision. The author is obliged to amend the article in accordance with the comments of the reviewers or the editorial board.

2. The editor must review all manuscripts which are submitted to the publication without prejudice, evaluating each and every one, regardless of race, religion, nationality, as well as the position or work place of the author (s). However, the editor may take into account the relationship of the manuscript currently under consideration with other articles submitted earlier by the same authors.

3. The editor should as soon as possible review the manuscripts submitted to publication.

4. All responsibility for accepting or rejecting the manuscript lies with the editor. Responsible and well-considered approach to fulfilling these obligations means that the editor takes into account the recommendation of the reviewer-doctor of sciences of corresponding scientific direction concerning the quality and authenticity of the manuscript which is submitted for publication. However, manuscripts may be rejected without a review if the editor believes that they do not fit the Journal profile.

5. The editor and editorial staff shall not disclose to other persons any information related to the content of the manuscript under consideration other than those who participate in the professional assessment of this manuscript. After a positive editorial decision, the article will be published in the Journal and posted on the appropriate electronic resources.

6. It is allowed to distribute any articles from the magazine or excerpts from them on electronic networks, but with such distribution the reference to the primary source is obligatory. Prohibited the publication and / or distribution of materials by third parties or organizations on paper and solid electronic media.

7. In accordance with international law, in respect of copyright compliance, electronic information resources, materials, electronic journal or project cannot be reproduced in whole or in part in any form (electronic or print) without the prior written consent of the authors and editorial staff of the Journal. When using published materials in the context of other documents, a reference to the original source is required.

8. The editor must respect the intellectual independence of the authors.

9. The responsibility and the rights of the editor of the Journal in relation to any submitted manuscript, the author of which is the editor itself, must be delegated to any other qualified person.

10. Unpublished information, arguments or interpretations disclosed in the manuscript submitted can be used by editors in their own research only with the permit of the author. If the manuscript is so closely related to current or past editorial research that a conflict of interest may arise, the editor must take steps to ensure that any other qualified person takes editorial responsibility for the manuscript.

11. If the editor presents convincing evidence that the main content or conclusions of the article published in the Journal are false, the editor should facilitate the publication of the corresponding message indicating this error and, if possible, correct it. This message may be written by the person who found this error, or by an independent author.

12. The author can require that the editor not use some reviewers when considering the manuscript. However, the editor may decide to use one or more of these reviewers if he knows that their thoughts are important for the impartial review of the manuscript. Such a decision can be made, for example, in the case of serious contradictions between this manuscript and the preliminary work of the potential reviewer.

The Ethical Code of authors

1. The main responsibility of the author is to provide an accurate record of the study, as well as an objective discussion of its significance.

2. The authors of the articles bear all responsibility for the content of the articles and for the fact of their publication. The editors of the Journal are not liable to the authors and / or third parties and organizations for any damage caused by the publication of the article. The editorial board has the right to remove an article that has already been published, if it turns out that during the publication of the article were violated someone's rights or generally accepted norms of scientific ethics. The fact that the article was removed from the article informs the author who provided the article, the person who recommended, and the organization where the research was performed.

3. The Journal volume is a limited resource, so the author must use it reasonably and economically.

4. The initial message of the research results should be sufficiently complete and contain the necessary references to available sources of information so that specialists in this area can repeat this work. If required, the author should make reasonable efforts to provide other researchers with samples of unusual materials that cannot be obtained in any other way; with the corresponding agreements on the transfer of materials that limit the use of such materials to protect the legitimate interests of the authors.

5. The author should cite those publications that have had a decisive influence on the nature of work, as well as those that can quickly introduce the reader to earlier work which are important to understanding this research. With the exception of reviews, you should minimize quoting works that are not directly related to this message. The author is required to conduct a literary search to find and cite original publications that describe the research, are closely related to this message. It is also necessary to specify the sources of material of essential importance used in this work, if these materials were not obtained by the author.

6. The work must be clearly indicated any dangerous manifestations and risks which can be associated with the research.

7. The author should avoid fragmentation of research messages. The author should arrange the publication in such way that each message gives a complete report on each aspect of the general study.

8. When preparing a manuscript for publication, the author must inform the editor of related manuscripts which are submitted or accepted for publication. Copies of these manuscripts must be submitted to the editor. The author must also indicate their links with the manuscript which is submitted for publication.

9. The author must not submit manuscripts that essentially describe the same results in more than one journal in the form of a primary publication, unless it is repeated by a journal rejected or revoked by the author of the manuscript. It is permited to submit a manuscript of a full article that expands the previously published short preliminary report (notice) about the same work. However, when providing such a manuscript, the editor must be notified of an earlier notice, and this prior notice must be quoted in this manuscript.

10. The author should clearly indicate the sources of all quoted or presented information, with the exception of well-known information. Information which was received privately, during a conversation, in correspondence or during discussions with third parties, should not be used or reported in the work of the author without the permission of the researcher from whom this information was received. Information which was obtained through the provision of confidential services, such as when reviewing manuscripts or projects submitted for grants, should be treated in the same way.

11. Experimental or theoretical research may sometimes serve as the basis for criticizing the work of another researcher. Published articles may contain such criticism, if it is appropriate. However, personal criticism can not be considered relevant in any cases.

12. The author, who represents the manuscript for publication, is responsible for including all the persons who meet the criterion of authorship in the list of co-authors. He submits to the editorial contact information, documents and reports to the editors and assumes responsibility for the consent of the other authors of the article for its publication in the journal.

13. Authors should inform the editor of any potential conflicts of interest, such as the consulting or financial interests of any company that might be affected by the publication of the results which are contained in this manuscript. Authors should ensure that there are no contractual relationships or property rights that could affect the publication of the information which is contained in submitted manuscript.

The Ethical Code of reviewers

1. The reviewer must objectively evaluate the quality of submitted manuscript to the experimental and theoretical work, its interpretation and presentation, as well as to take into account the extent to which the work corresponds to high scientific and literary standards. The reviewer must respect the intellectual independence of the authors.

2. The reviewer must take into account the possibility of a conflict of interest in cases when this manuscript is closely related to the current or published work of the reviewer. If in doubt, the reviewer must immediately return the manuscript without a review, indicating a conflict of interest.

3. The reviewer should not evaluate the manuscript, with the author or co-author of which he has personal or professional links, and if such relationships may affect the judgment of the manuscript.

4. The reviewer must deal with the manuscript which was sent to the review as a confidential document. He should not display the manuscript to other persons or discuss it with other colleagues, except those cases when the reviewer needs someone else's special advice.

5. Reviewers must adequately explain and justify their judgments for better understanding their position to editors and authors. Any allegation that observations, conclusions or arguments have already been published have to be accompanied by relevant references.

6. The reviewer must mark any cases of insufficient citation by the authors of other scholars works who are directly related to the work which are reviewed. Howewer, the comments of the reviewer to insufficient citation of his research by the author may be looking biased. The reviewer should draw the attention of the editor to any significant similarity between the given manuscript and any published article or any manuscript which is submitted simultaneously to another journal.

7. The reviewer must provide feedback the review in a timely.

8. Reviewers should not use or disclose unpublished information, arguments, or interpretations which are contained in the manuscript unless otherwise authorized by the author. However, when such information indicates that some of the reviewer's own research may prove to be unsuccessful, the termination of such work by the reviewer is not contrary to ethical standards.
